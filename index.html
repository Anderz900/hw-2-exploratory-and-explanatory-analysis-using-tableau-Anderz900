Explanation


I actually wanted to combine both the story structure on the Euribor Rate graph and then mesh that into a dashboard alongside the rest of the graphs. However, I could not figure out how to do that so I instead opted out for having nice symmetry (with color) with both the scatterplot and bar graphs on the right. I believed this was the much better option because it created stability and beauty into the design. In addition, I think it is worth discussing my favorite graphs to avoid confusion. The scatterplot implies higher subscription with less than 5 calls as the data portrays the more dense "yes" area ocurring below the constant 5 line. The Euribor rate is akin to the Libor (London Interbank Rate) and is the rate at which banks in Europe lend to each other. The Euribor rate is also similar in effect to the Federal Funds Rate in America but is computed differently. Evidently, the annotations in the graph tell the rest of the story. Lastly, as the bar graph on the right has been taking data over the same time period, the percentage of people who said "yes" compared to "no" is analogous to describing the rate of growth. It is highly important therefore to understand that students could be a major source of clientele in the future. It is also worth noting that the ordering of the elements in the legend plays a huge part in displaying data. Below, one can see the drastic effects on clarity it can have by emphasizing "no" over "yes".
